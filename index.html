~ About Canada Type Leather ~

Leather was designed by Patrick Griffin in 2004. Published by Canada Type, it was designed as a blackletter font that can be used in a variety of common contemporary design approaches, such as minimal, geometric, modular, etc. Leather is a digitization and major expansion of Imre Reiner’s 1933 Gotika design. Back then, this design saw very little use because it caused problems for printers, where the thin serifs and inner bars were too fragile and

broke off too easily when used in metal. Inspired by classical blackletter, the most defining feature of Leather is its details and number of strokes that deviate from more mainstream type faces. Most characters feature many additional thick and thin strokes that cross diagonally, horizontally and vertically, with sharp angles and few curves. This gives Leather its modern Grid-based geometric and angular beauty, while referencing its more classical rounded blackletter calligraphy style.
